Role reversal — when support becomes resistance — 5 questions

Question 1 of 5
What is "role reversal" in technical analysis?
AWhen a stock switches from a growth stock to a value stock
BWhen a support level becomes resistance (or resistance becomes support) after price breaks through it
CWhen buyers and sellers swap their positions in a trade
DWhen the trend changes direction from up to down
Exactly right. Role reversal is when a level switches its function — a support level that gets broken then acts as resistance on the way back up, and vice versa.
Role reversal refers specifically to S&R levels switching their function. When price breaks through a support level, that level often becomes resistance — and when resistance breaks, it often becomes support.
Question 2 of 5
Why does a broken support level often become resistance when price returns to it?
ABecause the stock exchange marks it as a resistance level in their system
BBecause traders who bought at the support level are now in a losing trade and want to sell when price returns to their entry
CBecause market makers deliberately push price back down at that level
DBecause support levels always become resistance after exactly 30 days
Correct. Traders who bought at the old support are now sitting on a loss. When price returns to their entry level, many of them sell to cut their losses or exit at breakeven — creating selling pressure that turns the level into resistance.
It's about trader psychology. Buyers who got trapped at the support level are in losing positions. When price comes back to that level, they sell to exit — and that collective selling pressure turns the old floor into a ceiling.
Question 3 of 5
Price breaks above a resistance level at $80, rallies to $95, then pulls back. What do you expect to happen when price reaches $80 again?
APrice will automatically drop back below $80 as the old resistance reasserts itself
BThe $80 level is now irrelevant and should be ignored
CThe $80 level may now act as support — buyers stepping in where sellers previously were
DPrice will gap through $80 without any reaction
Correct. Old resistance at $80 becomes potential new support. Traders who missed the initial breakout may look to buy on the pullback to $80 — and short sellers who were wrong may close their positions there, adding buying pressure.
When resistance breaks and price pulls back to that level, it often acts as new support. Buyers who missed the breakout see the pullback as an opportunity, and trapped short sellers close positions there — both creating buying pressure at $80.
Question 4 of 5
Which of these is the best confirmation that a role reversal has occurred?
APrice briefly dips below support by one or two cents before recovering
BA convincing break through the level, a meaningful move away, then a retest that respects the level in its new role
CThe stock's trading volume doubles on the day of the break
DThe level has been in place for more than six months
Spot on. A true role reversal requires three things: a convincing break (not a fakeout), a meaningful move away from the level, and then a retest where price respects the level in its new role — stalling and reversing at that point.
A brief dip below support that immediately recovers is a fakeout, not a break. True role reversal confirmation needs: a convincing break, a significant move away, and then a retest where the level holds in its new role.
Question 5 of 5
Using the floor-and-ceiling analogy: if you fall through the floor of a building, what does the floor become from your new perspective below?
AIt becomes the ground — no longer relevant
BIt stays a floor — nothing changes
CIt becomes the ceiling — the same surface now acts as a barrier above you
DIt becomes a wall — the level now blocks sideways movement
Perfect. The floor from above is the ceiling from below — same surface, completely different role depending on which side of it you're on. That's exactly how role reversal works on a price chart.
The correct answer is ceiling. Once you fall through the floor, that same surface is now above you — it's become your ceiling. On a price chart: fall through support, and that level is now resistance above price. Same surface, new role.
