Reading a candlestick chart

Click each button to explore a different concept

The anatomy of a candlestick. Every candle packs four data points into one shape: open, close, high, and low. The body (thick part) shows the open-to-close range. The wicks (thin lines) show the high and low extremes reached during that period.
A bullish (green) candle. The close is higher than the open — buyers pushed price up during this period. The longer the body, the more convincing the move. A long upper wick means buyers tried to go higher but sellers pushed back near the top.
A bearish (red) candle. The close is lower than the open — sellers were in control. A long lower wick means sellers pushed price down hard, but buyers stepped back in before the close. Always read the whole candle, not just the colour.
Reading a series of candles. A run of green candles = buyers in control (uptrend). A run of red candles = sellers in control (downtrend). Watch for where one side runs out of steam — that's often near a support or resistance level.
