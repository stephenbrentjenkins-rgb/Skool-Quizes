What do the four data points in a candlestick represent?
APrice, volume, momentum, and trend direction
BOpen, close, high, and low
CBuy price, sell price, average price, and daily change
DSupport, resistance, breakout, and reversal levels
Correct. Every candle shows the open (where it started), close (where it ended), high (peak reached), and low (lowest point). Those four points tell the whole story of that period.
Not quite. A candlestick always shows four things: open, close, high, and low. These four data points pack the full story of a time period into one shape.
Question 2 of 5
A candlestick is green. What does this tell you?
AThe stock is a good buy right now
BThe stock closed higher than it opened — buyers were in control during that period
CThe price reached its highest point of the year
DThe stock is in a long-term uptrend
Exactly right. Green simply means the close was higher than the open. Buyers won that particular period. It doesn't tell you about the overall trend — just that one period.
Green tells you only one thing: the price closed higher than it opened for that period. Buyers were in control. It says nothing about the trend, valuation, or whether it's a good trade.
Question 3 of 5
What do the thin lines (wicks) above and below a candle body represent?
AThe trading volume for that period
BThe previous day's open and close
CThe high and low price reached during the period
DThe support and resistance levels
Correct. The wicks show the full price range — how far up price got (upper wick) and how far down it went (lower wick) before ending the period. Long wicks are full of meaning.
The wicks show the high and low of the period. The upper wick = the highest price reached. The lower wick = the lowest price reached. They show the extremes that the body doesn't capture.
Question 4 of 5
Why is the daily chart recommended for beginners when learning support and resistance?
AIt updates faster than other timeframes
BIt filters out short-term noise and shows the most reliable S&R levels
CIt's the only chart available on free platforms
DIt shows price in real time
Spot on. The daily chart smooths out short-term noise and shows you the levels that a large number of traders are watching — which makes those levels more significant and more reliable.
The daily chart is recommended because it filters out the noisy short-term fluctuations and reveals the support and resistance levels that the most traders are watching — making them more reliable.
Question 5 of 5
A stock is making higher highs and higher lows on its chart. What does this describe?
AA downtrend — sellers are in control
BA sideways market — neither buyers nor sellers are winning
CAn uptrend — buyers are in control
DA reversal — the trend is about to change
Perfect. Higher highs and higher lows = uptrend. Buyers are pushing price to new peaks, and even the pullbacks are ending at higher levels than before. Buyers are firmly in control.
Higher highs and higher lows define an uptrend — not a downtrend or sideways move. Each rally goes higher than the last, and each dip ends higher than the last. That's a market where buyers are in control.
