Lesson 8 · Capstone

Your S&R trading playbook

Use these interactive tools in your daily trading routine

1
Build your watchlist (20–30 stocks)
Choose stocks you know well. Familiarity matters — you need to recognise when a chart is approaching a significant level, not discover it for the first time under pressure.
2
Scan every morning (15 minutes)
Before the market opens, go through your list. Ask: is price approaching a key S&R level? Is there confluence (RSI, MA, volume)? Is the R:R at least 2:1? Three yesses = goes on your active radar.
3
Set alerts for active setups
On TradingView, set a price alert just above your S&R level. When price approaches, you get notified and check for your confirmation signal. You don't need to watch screens all day.
4
Quality over quantity
One high-conviction setup per week is better than five marginal ones. Most weeks you may not find anything that meets all criteria. That patience is not weakness — it's discipline.

1
Drawing too many levels
If your chart is covered in horizontal lines, start over. Two or three well-tested, obvious levels. More lines equals more confusion, not more edge.
2
Treating levels as exact prices
Support at $50 means the zone around $50 — not $50.00 precisely. Draw zones, not lines. Price will poke through by a few cents before reversing. That's normal — your stop should account for it.
3
Chasing trades
Missed the entry? Let it go. Entering late destroys your risk-to-reward ratio and teaches your brain that impulsive trades are acceptable. There will always be another setup.
4
Fighting the trend
Always check the 50 MA before entering. Trading in the direction of the trend significantly increases your win rate. Counter-trend trades require more skill and experience — save them for later.
5
Skipping the trade plan
Every single trade. Written out before you enter. No exceptions. A plan keeps you honest, prevents emotional decisions, and gives you something concrete to review in your journal.
