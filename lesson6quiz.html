Lesson 6 Knowledge Check

Combining S&R with indicators — 5 questions

Question 1 of 5
Price is approaching a support level and the RSI reading is 28. What does this tell you?
AThe support level is about to break — RSI below 30 means the selling is too strong
BTwo signals are aligning — price is at a key buying level AND momentum is oversold, increasing the probability of a bounce
CRSI below 30 means you should always sell, regardless of the S&R level
DThe RSI reading is irrelevant — only the S&R level matters
Correct. RSI near 30 (oversold) at a support level is classic confluence. Both indicators are pointing in the same direction — momentum is stretched to the downside at a historically significant buying level. That's a high-probability bounce setup.
RSI below 30 doesn't mean sell — it means oversold, which combined with support means the opposite. Two signals aligning (price at support + RSI oversold) creates confluence, increasing the probability of a bounce higher.
Question 2 of 5
What does it mean when the 50-day moving average coincides with a horizontal support level?
AThe support level is now invalid and should be ignored
BIt creates a strong confluence zone — two different reasons for buyers to show up at the same price
CIt means price will definitely bounce — this setup never fails
DMoving averages and S&R levels should never be used together
Exactly right. When a horizontal support zone and the 50 MA overlap, you have two completely different reasons for buying pressure to emerge at the same price. These confluence zones tend to produce the strongest, most reliable reactions.
When the 50 MA sits at a horizontal S&R level, it creates confluence — two different analytical methods pointing to the same area as significant. More reasons for buyers to show up = stronger likely reaction. No setup is guaranteed, but this increases probability.
Question 3 of 5
A stock breaks above resistance on very low volume. What should you think?
ALow volume breakouts are more reliable because there are fewer sellers in the way
BThis is a strong breakout — volume doesn't matter once price crosses the level
CBe cautious — low volume breakouts are a common warning sign for fakeouts, as the move lacks broad market participation
DImmediately buy — any break above resistance is a strong buy signal
Correct. Low volume on a breakout is a red flag. If the move had real conviction behind it, you'd expect elevated volume as more market participants jump in. Low volume suggests thin participation — the move is vulnerable to reversal. Be cautious or wait for volume confirmation.
Low volume breakouts are a warning sign, not a green light. A genuine breakout should attract participants — which shows up as elevated volume. Low volume means few traders are actually behind the move, making it more likely to reverse (fakeout).
Question 4 of 5
What does an RSI reading above 70 tell you when price is approaching resistance?
AThe stock has strong momentum and will definitely break through resistance
BRSI above 70 is a buy signal — the stock is strong
CThe stock is overbought at resistance — both signals suggest sellers may step in and push price back down
DRSI above 70 means the stock is undervalued and cheap to buy
Correct. RSI above 70 (overbought) at a resistance level is powerful confluence for a rejection setup. Momentum is stretched to the upside AND price is at a historically significant selling level. Both signals point to increased probability of a pullback.
RSI above 70 means overbought — which at resistance is a warning, not a buy signal. Momentum is stretched just as price reaches a level where sellers historically show up. Both signals agree: the rally may be running out of steam. Watch for rejection.
Question 5 of 5
You find a setup with price at support, RSI at 32, and the 50 MA nearby — but volume is very low. What do you do?
AEnter immediately — three signals is more than enough
BSkip it — you need all four signals to align before entering any trade
CEnter with reduced size or wait to see if volume picks up — three signals are good but low volume is a notable concern
DIgnore the volume — it only matters on breakout trades, not bounce trades
Well reasoned. Three confluence signals is good, but low volume on a bounce is a concern — it means fewer participants are behind the move. Entering with reduced position size or waiting for volume to confirm are both sensible approaches. You don't need all four signals every time, but each missing one lowers your conviction.
Three signals is good, but volume matters on bounce trades too — it confirms that real buying pressure is behind the move. With low volume, consider entering smaller than normal or waiting for a volume pickup before committing fully. Manage the uncertainty rather than ignoring it.
