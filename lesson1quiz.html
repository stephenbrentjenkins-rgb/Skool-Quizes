What is a support level?

BA price level where buyers consistently show up and push price up

DA government guarantee on a stock's minimum value

Question 2 of 5
A stock bounces off the $80 level four times over six months. What does this tell you?
AThe stock is broken and you should avoid it
B$80 is a weak level because the price keeps returning to it
C$80 is a strong support level — many traders are watching and buying at that price
DThe level doesn't matter until the stock breaks through it
Correct. More touches = stronger level. Four bounces means a lot of buyers have clustered at $80 — it's a level worth watching closely.
The opposite is true — more touches make a level stronger, not weaker. Four bounces signals that many traders see $80 as a key buying opportunity.
Question 3 of 5
Why do support and resistance levels exist in the first place?
AThey are set by stock exchanges as official price limits
BThey are calculated using complex mathematical formulas
CTraders remember past prices and make decisions based on where price has reacted before
DThey are created by large hedge funds to trap small traders
Spot on. S&R levels are a product of collective trader memory and psychology — not formulas or rules. That's what makes them so powerful.
S&R levels come from trader psychology. When many traders remember a price reacting at a certain level, they make similar decisions there in the future — creating the level again.
Question 4 of 5
If a stock is approaching a resistance level, what is most likely to happen?
AThe stock will always break through the level and keep rising
BSellers may step in and push the price back down — the price "hits the ceiling"
CThe stock price will freeze and stop moving entirely
DBuyers will rush in, causing a guaranteed rally
Correct. At a resistance level, sellers tend to show up in force — pushing the price back down. It acts like a ceiling.
Resistance levels are ceilings where sellers gather. The most likely outcome is that selling pressure increases and price gets pushed back down — though breakouts do happen too.
Question 5 of 5

AFoundation
BWall
CCeiling

Perfect. Support = floor, Resistance = ceiling. Those two analogies are the foundation of everything we'll build on from here.
The correct analogy is ceiling. Support is the floor (price bounces up), resistance is the ceiling (price gets pushed down).
